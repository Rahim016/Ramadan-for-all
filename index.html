What is Ramadan?

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. From dawn to sunset, Muslims refrain from eating, drinking, and other physical needs to strengthen their faith and practice self-discipline. It is also a time for increased charity, worship, and spiritual growth, culminating in the celebration of Eid al-Fitr at the end of the month.


What are the Virtues of Ramadan


Some of the key virtues include:

Spiritual Cleansing: Fasting during Ramadan helps purify the soul, encouraging self-discipline, empathy, and gratitude.
Increased Worship: Muslims engage in extra prayers, including Taraweeh at night, to deepen their connection with God (Allah).
Compassion and Charity: Ramadan emphasizes helping others, particularly the less fortunate. Acts of charity and kindness are encouraged.
Self-Discipline:: Fasting from dawn to sunset teaches self-control over desires and helps individuals grow spiritually and mentally.
Solidarity: Ramadan fosters a sense of unity as Muslims around the world fast and worship together, strengthening the global Muslim community.

Method of Fasting

Fasting during Ramadan is a spiritual practice where Muslims abstain from food, drink, smoking, and marital relations from dawn (Fajr) to sunset (Maghrib). It begins with the pre-dawn meal, Suhoor, and ends with Iftar, breaking the fast at sunset, often with dates and water. Fasting encourages self-discipline, empathy for the less fortunate, and closeness to Allah through prayer, reciting the Qur'an, and reflection. Exemptions apply to the elderly, sick, pregnant/nursing women, children, and travelers. Charity (Zakat) is encouraged during Ramadan to purify wealth and assist those in need.

The Taraweeh prayer is a speacial prayer unique to the month of Ramadan. In addition to the five daily prayers Muslims perform a prayer during the nights of Ramadan called the taraweeh prayer. It consists of 20 units of prayer followed by three units of the witr prayer. It is performed directly after the final prayer of the day (Isha). Traditionally the Imam recites an entire chapter of the Holy Qur'an each night during the taraweeh prayer enabling them to complete an entire recitation of the Qur'an by the end of Ramadan!
